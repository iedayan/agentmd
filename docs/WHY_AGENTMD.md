# Why AgentMD: The Problem We Solve

The problems AgentMD solves are widely recognized and actively hindering enterprise AI adoption. This document summarizes the evidence and how AgentMD addresses each gap.

---

## Problem 1: Agent Sprawl & Integration Chaos

Enterprises are drowning in disconnected AI agents, creating fragmentation that slows adoption and increases risk.

| Statistic | Source |
|-----------|--------|
| Organizations use an average of **12 agents today**, expected to reach **20 by 2027** | Salesforce/Deloitte |
| **83%** of organizations report most teams have adopted AI agents | Salesforce |
| **~50% of AI agents operate in isolated silos** vs. part of multi-agent systems | Salesforce |
| **>4 in 5 IT leaders** believe AI agents will create more complexity than value due to integration challenges | CIO Dive |
| Average organization manages **957 applications**, but only **27% are connected** | Salesforce |
| **64% of IT leaders** express concerns about meeting AI implementation goals | CIO Dive |
| **35%** cite integrating siloed apps/data as primary hurdle | CIO Dive |

**Why this matters:** Agents working in silos lead to disjointed workflows, redundant automations, and shadow AI risk.

**How AgentMD helps:** Integration with the AGENTS.md standard provides a unified way for agents to understand and execute workflows. One file, one spec, all compatible agents (Claude, Cursor, Copilot, etc.).
